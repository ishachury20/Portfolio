Communication about Education

Understanding New Technologies

EduConnect allows teachers and parents to communicate and remain updated about various types of technologies, both inside and outside of the classroom.

Online communities play a crucial role in forming and maintaining relationships, but they can also negatively impact mental well-being. This study focuses on mitigating these negative effects by promoting prosocial behavior through the concept of a "gratitude cycle." The aim is to understand how digital platforms can be designed to reduce negativity and cultivate a more supportive and uplifting online environment.

Project Background

This project was completed under the guidance of Professor David Lee in the CMPM 178 - Human Centered Design Research course. As students learning the design research process, we conducted interviews with the PVUSD community, and performed the entire design research process from qualitative analysis to brainstorming, ideation, and creating a fully-designed solution to real world user needs. This project focused on communication between the teaching staff and parents around various technologies used or referenced in class.

Needfinding

Understanding New Technologies

To begin our project, we spent the first two weeks conducting a qualitative analysis of the given transcript of user interviews within the Parajo Valley High School District. We qualitatively coded, analyzed, and conducted thematic analyses to find themes within each user group. Based on these themes, we also created problem statements formed by specific insights from each user group to better identify and understand the core needs of each group. For the sake of relevancy within this poster, we will showcase the insights found within our target user group, parents within the Parajo Valley District.
